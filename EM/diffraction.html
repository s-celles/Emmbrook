Panel 1: Light scattering from a subwavelength array

Assume that there are \( N \) scatterers, uniformly spaced by \( a \), with total length \( (N-1) a \): a finite lattice.
The lattice could be a diffraction grating made by optical lithography (probed by visible light, like a laser pointer) or a crystal lattice (probed by x-rays or electron waves, as in a transmission electron microscope.)
The simulation assumes that each scatterer is a point source for a spherical light wave, radiating outwards from the point.)
You will see that the diffraction pattern images the Fourier transform of the lattice. Since atomic spacings (periodicities) are characteristic of elements, this allows us to determine a material's composition.
