Transfer matrix

The transfer matrix \( M \) for optics relates the electric field \( E \) and magnetic field \( H \) at different points for a propagating EM wave. We let the radiation propagate along \( \hat{ \mathbf{ z } } \) and have polarization on \( \hat{ \mathbf{ x } } \). The medium is bounded on the left \( -z \) by medium \( L \) and on the right \( z > d \) by medium \( R \). The fields are related at points \( z = 0 \) and \( z = d \) through $$ \begin{bmatrix} E(d) \\ H(d) \end{bmatrix} = M(\varepsilon, \mu, d) \begin{bmatrix} E(0) \\ H(0) \end{bmatrix}, $$ where $$ M(\varepsilon, \mu, z) = \begin{bmatrix} \cos(k z) & \frac{ i }{ p } \sin(k z) \\ i p \sin(k z) & \cos(k z) \end{bmatrix} $$ is called transfer matrix, \( k = 2\pi / \lambda \) is the wavenumber, and \( p \) characterizes the medium by $$ p = \sqrt{ \frac{ \varepsilon }{ \mu } } $$ as shown above.

We can express reflection and transmission coefficients \( r \) and \( t \) in terms of \( M \). For waves incident from one side only, (e.g. from the top, propagating towards \( +z \), we have $$ \begin{bmatrix} t\\ p_R t \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \cos(k z) & \frac{ i }{ p } \sin(k z) \\ i p \sin(k z) & \cos(k z) \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 + r\\ p_L (1 - r) \end{bmatrix}. $$

The intensity ratios \( R \) and \( T \) are given by \( R = | r |^2 \) and \( T = | t |^2 \).
